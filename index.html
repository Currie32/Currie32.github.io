About Me

I will make a difference in this world.

Having earned a Bachelor of Commerce from McGill University, I believe that I have an excellent foundation to succeed. While studying there, I was a member of the Baja Racing Team, an excellent student-run organization that builds a race car each year and competes in competitions around Canada and the United States. In my final year, I served as the Administrative Director, and helped the team reach new feats. We won numerous awards as a result of the hard work and dedication of my teammates and I, and finished 4th out of more than 100 universities from around the globe in the Society of Automotive Engineer (SAE) competitions, the three official competitions.

In recent years, I have been a member of three exceptional organization: Chestnut Lake Camp, Chungdahm Learning, and Timber Lake Camp. Working primarily with children, I have had the rewarding experience of teaching children responsibility, life-long skills, and how to make and keep friends. At Timber Lake Camp, where I served as a Division Leader, I really enjoyed using my experience to help improve my counsellors' abilities. Eight out of my eleven counsellors earned the top evaluation grade as a result of our dedication to helping each other, and most importantly the campers, to grow.

Over the past two years, I have been learning to program, primarily with Udacity. Having grown my interest over this time, I wanted to do some structured learning to really boost my skill set. This is why I worked diligently during September and October, 2016, to earn a Data Analyst Nanodegree from Udacity. Data analysis and machine learning are fascinating fields that will become increasingly more useful and I will continue to learn more about them.

With my new education and enthusiasm, I am ready to help people and businesses succeed. Let's build something great. insanely great.
